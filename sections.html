Tax Saving Sections

Section 80C

Maximum Deduction: ₹1,50,000

Eligible Investments: PPF, EPF, ELSS, NSC, etc.

Section 80D

Maximum Deduction: ₹25,000 (₹50,000 for senior citizens)

Eligible Expenses: Health insurance premiums, preventive health check-ups.

Section 24(b)

Maximum Deduction: ₹2,00,000

Eligible Expenses: Home loan interest payments.

Section 80E

Maximum Deduction: No limit, for up to 8 years.

Eligible Expenses: Interest on education loans.

Section 80G

Maximum Deduction: 50% or 100% of donation.

Eligible Donations: Donations to specified charitable institutions and relief funds.

Section 80CCD(1B)

Maximum Deduction: ₹50,000

Eligible Contributions: Contributions to NPS.

Section 80TTA/80TTB

Maximum Deduction: ₹10,000 for savings accounts (₹50,000 for senior citizens)

Eligible Interest: Interest on savings accounts and fixed deposits.

Section 80U

Maximum Deduction: ₹75,000 (₹1,25,000 for severe disability)

Eligible Individuals: Individuals with disabilities.

Section 10(14): HRA

Eligible Expenses: House Rent Allowance (HRA) received by salaried employees.

Conditions: Rent should exceed 10% of salary.

Section 80GG

Maximum Deduction: ₹60,000 per year

Eligible Expenses: Rent paid by individuals who do not receive HRA.
